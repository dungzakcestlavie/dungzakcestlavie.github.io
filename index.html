DUNGZAK CESTLAVIE is a Korean contemporary artist whose work explores the origins of light, memory, and being. His practice centers on documenting the traces of human emotion, the luminous core that emerges through wounds, and the cyclical rhythm of disappearance and renewal within existence.

Building a multi-media artistic world that integrates painting, text, sound, and archival research, he works primarily from Seoul while expanding his projects across Berlin, Paris, Amsterdam, Warsaw, and Moscow. His paintings function as both prayer and record, articulating a language of light that resurrects the emotional roots embedded in the quiet spaces of existence.
